Topology notes — LinkedIn (SEAN4128)

Evidence volume is not evidence balance. Teams get into trouble when a dense cluster of results in one part of the stack is treated as a green light for the whole system. That pattern inflates confidence locally and then leaks it globally.

Start at the core. Frame the question and goal, then represent the evidence as structured objects. Hand that to a decision layer that arbitrates across mechanistic, genetic, and clinical structures. Cross‑evidence judgment is where “should we advance?” is actually decided, not in the noisiest corner of the data.

Keep asymmetry explicit. Strong human signal without mechanistic or genetic support is fragile. Strong mechanism plus genetics without human closure is constrained. Topology treats transfer as a gated property: confidence only propagates when the next layer’s gate is shut with fit‑for‑purpose evidence.

Use the overlay last. Its job is to translate a settled decision object into strategy that humans can read. If the overlay begins to add “new” evidence or relitigate the structure, you’ve inverted the stack and turned interpretation into speculation.

Practice loop: Core (build the structured evidence) → Decision (arbitrate across layers, surface tensions) → Overlay (render the strategy). This keeps uncertainty where it belongs, prevents local coherence from masquerading as validation, and makes decisions legible without pretending that missing layers are solved.
