Topology notes — treating confidence as layer-dependent

A recurring failure mode in scientific interpretation is assuming that agreement inside one evidence layer implies global readiness. Signal density in a mechanistic cluster or a clean genetic association can reshape local geometry, yet leave overall transfer potential unchanged.

Omega Infinity’s stack keeps the ordering strict: SYC core for orchestration, the decision layer for cross-evidence arbitration, and the overlay for interpretation. This sequence matters. Structure precedes narrative; decisions are formed on topology, not on the persuasiveness of prose.

Evidence-layer asymmetry is the rule, not the exception. Mechanistic plausibility without genetic reinforcement, or genetic signal without clinical maturation, creates partial‑stack convergence—not validation. Confidence propagates only when gates are closed across layers; otherwise it remains local.

Operationally: articulate the question and goal, submit structured mechanistic/genetic/clinical objects to the decision layer, then request the overlay for a human‑readable summary. This preserves stack integrity, makes uncertainty visible at the right layer, and reduces the temptation to resolve ambiguity with narrative.

As evidence accumulates, focus on balance, not volume: where does support fail to transfer, and what would close that gap? Treat transfer as a gated property and let the topology set the pace of interpretation.
